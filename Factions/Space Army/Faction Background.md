# Space Army

## Overview

The entity humanity knows as the "Space Army" is not a nation, not an alliance of equals, and not a unified military force in any recognizable sense. It is a hierarchy of conquest — a predatory structure built from species subjugated, weaponized, and discarded by the Venusians, the apex civilization of the outer system. Humanity named them the Invaders upon first contact, a label that survived because it was accurate enough. What humanity did not immediately understand was that the Invaders it was fighting were not the ones in charge.

At the top sits Venus. Below Venus, in descending order of expendability, are the client races — conquered species pressed into service as soldiers, scouts, advisors, and, when they cease to be useful, food. The Martians occupy a peculiar niche in this arrangement: too clever to consume cheaply, too alien to fully trust, and possessed of information that Venusian commanders need. They survive through indispensability, and they engineer that indispensability with the same precision they apply to everything else.

## Ideology & Goals

The Venusians do not have ideology. They have appetite. The consumption and subjugation of other species is not policy — it is ecology. Lower species exist to serve Venusian purposes or to feed Venusian populations. The concept of coexistence is not dismissed so much as it is categorically absent from their framework.

The goal, with respect to Earth, is eventual conquest. The operative word is *eventual*. Earth has proven to be an unusually violent and inventive world. Its inhabitants maintain a perpetual state of internal conflict that, paradoxically, accelerates the development of weapons technology. Every invasion attempt has encountered not an unprepared prey species but a civilization already sharpened by centuries of war against itself. The Venusians are patient. They have time. Earth does not need to be taken today.

The Martians have a different goal. Their goal is tomorrow.

## Origin & History

### The Venusians

The oldest known spacefaring civilization in the inner system, the Venusians predate recorded human history. Their expansion was not a project — it was a tendency, the same way water flows downward. Species they encountered were assessed for utility and absorbed or eliminated accordingly. Several client races in the current coalition have no memory of a time before Venusian rule. The coalition's name is an artifact of human classification; the Venusians call it nothing in particular because it has never needed a name.

### The Martians

Mars was absorbed early. The Martians are small, physically unimposing, and would have been a footnote in Venusian expansion were it not for one thing: they are extraordinarily good at understanding other species. They observe, model, and predict with a consistency that the Venusians found useful and, eventually, indispensable.

What saved the Martians was not strength. It was the realization, arrived at collectively and without formal declaration, that information was the one resource the Venusians could not simply take. Knowledge could be withheld. Context could be omitted. A report could be technically accurate and strategically ruinous. The Martians began practicing this art quietly, and have never stopped.

Their integration into the Space Army was gradual. High-ranking Martian officers were embedded in Venusian command structures. When their counsel succeeded, the credit flowed upward. When it failed, the blame — and sometimes the Martian who gave the advice — flowed down. This arrangement persists because the Venusians have not found a way around it and because the Martians have been careful to ensure they never will.

### The Morden Alliance

At some point in the years preceding the current conflict, Martian agents made contact with the Rebel Army — the largest human insurgent force on Earth, known to its enemies as the Morden Army after its commander, Donald Morden. The alliance was practical on both sides: the Martians wanted intelligence and a foothold; the Rebel Army wanted technology. The exchange worked, for a time.

Donald Morden was many things, but he was not naive. He identified the signs of another Martian betrayal — they had turned on prior partners before — and began preparing to sever the arrangement on his own schedule. A complicating factor emerged: the Professor, a human scientist embedded in Martian circles, became a variable Morden could not fully account for. The alliance collapsed earlier than either side intended. The separation was not clean.

## Structure & Command

The Space Army's formal command structure is Venusian. Its functional command structure is more complicated.

Venusian commanders hold rank. Martian officers advise. In practice, a Venusian commander who ignores Martian advice tends not to win campaigns, and losing campaigns has consequences in a culture that reads failure as inadequacy. The dependency is real even when it is resented. Some Venusian commanders have developed quiet protective instincts toward their Martian advisors — not from affection, but from the recognition that dead advisors give worse counsel.

The Martians exploit this. They identify which Venusian commanders are genuinely shielding them and which are performing tolerance for political reasons. They cultivate the former and feed the latter information calibrated to produce results that advance Martian interests without obviously doing so.

Beneath this principal tension, other client races fill out the coalition. Their relationships with the Martians vary. Some species, newly absorbed, view Martians as peers in subjugation and extend a cautious solidarity. Others, longer in Venusian service, have internalized enough of the hierarchy to treat Martians with contempt. A small number are simply dangerous and see Martians as prey. The Martians maintain diplomatic relationships with all of them, playing a longer game than any individual commander can see.

### Notable Members

- **[[The-Professor]]** — Human scientist, foremost Earth expert in Martian service. Former Morden Army researcher. Clone-sustained. The Martians' most exposed and most valuable agent.
- **[[Invader-King]]** — Supreme Venusian authority. The consolidating force behind the coalition's current structure. Treats the Martian arrangement as a necessary inconvenience.

## Military Doctrine

The Space Army does not fight like a human military. It does not have logistics in any conventional sense, does not maintain supply lines, and does not experience morale collapse the way organic armies do. What it has is hierarchy and replaceable assets.

Client race units are deployed first, absorb attrition, and report on enemy capabilities. This is not callousness — it is methodology. The data gathered by expendable forces informs Venusian command decisions. Martian advisors interpret the data and translate it into recommendations. The recommendations filter up, get implemented or ignored, and the cycle continues.

Against Earth specifically, this approach has yielded mixed results. Human armies do not behave consistently. They adapt, innovate under pressure, and have demonstrated a recurring capacity to field weapons that should not exist given their apparent technological baseline. The Regular Army in particular has proven difficult to model. The Martians know more about this than they have shared.

## Relations with Other Factions

**Regular Army:** The primary obstacle to Earth conquest. The Martians hold more information about the Regular Army's capabilities than any other party. None of it has been fully disclosed to Venusian command.

**Rebel Army:** The former alliance is dead. Its legacy complicates both sides. Morden's forces now treat the Space Army with the same hostility they direct at the Regular Army, though for different reasons. The technology exchanged during the alliance exists on both sides. The Martians are aware of exactly what Morden knows. They have not shared this with the Venusians either.

**Other Client Races:** The coalition is not monolithic. Martians are actively cultivating factions within the coalition — species sympathetic enough, resentful enough, or pragmatic enough to be useful in an eventual restructuring of the hierarchy. This project is patient, measured, and entirely invisible to Venusian command.

## Trivia

- The term "Invaders" was coined in the first human intelligence reports following initial contact. The Martians have found it moderately useful as a label — it implies a unified force when the reality is considerably messier.
- Venusian commanders have been known to deny publicly that their Martian advisors' recommendations influenced a given battle decision, even when they were determinative. The Martians track these denials. They are useful leverage.
- The average Martian's lifespan under Venusian rule is longer than the average Martian's lifespan on pre-conquest Mars. The Venusians are aware of this. The Martians are aware that the Venusians are aware of this. Neither side discusses it.
