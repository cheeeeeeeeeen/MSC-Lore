## History / Origin

He has no name on record. The people who know him — Martians, former Rebel Army contacts, the occasional Venusian commander who tolerates his presence — call him the Professor, and the title has become the man.

He worked under the Rebel Army as a researcher during the period of Donald Morden's insurgency. The Rebel Army — known officially to its enemies as the Morden Army, after its commander — was the largest human insurgent force on Earth at the time of Martian first contact. The Professor was among the humans assigned to facilitate the exchange. Something in the contact changed him. He abandoned his employment without formal severance — Rebel Army records mark this as desertion — and embedded himself in Martian circles, learning their technology from the inside and applying it for his own purposes and theirs. Never for Morden.

The Regular Army's defeat of both the Morden forces and the subsequent Martian incursion killed him. Before the end, he completed one project for himself: a cloning apparatus capable of resuming the Professor, memory mostly intact, across an indefinite number of deaths.

The number is not, in fact, indefinite. Only he knows this.

## Personality

The Professor has never chosen anything on principle. He followed his curiosity into Martian service, not ideology. He did not betray the Morden Army out of loyalty to a competing cause — the technology was simply more interesting than his employment. He cloned himself not from a will to survive but because he found the problem tractable and solved it.

This is the question he has begun to pose to himself, quietly, across multiple clone cycles: whether a person assembled from curiosity, convenience, and survival instinct can develop values. He does not yet know the answer. The Venusian hierarchy is slowly constructing a situation where he may find out.

Each new clone wakes up and reads his own notes. He knows the history — Morden, the Martians, the defeats, the cloning — but he cannot fully verify how much of it is genuine memory and how much is reconstruction artifact from the transfer. He carries a biography he cannot confirm as his own. His relationship with the Martians becomes quietly complicated by this: are they trusting him, or the reputation of whoever he was before?

## Role in the Conflict

Most exposed Martian asset in the field. His clone series makes him expendable in ways Martian personnel are not — he absorbs operational risk by accepting assignments that would threaten irreplaceable Martian officers. He knows this. He finds it pragmatic rather than demeaning.

His Earth expertise is unmatched within the coalition. He knows the Regular Army, the Rebel Army, human psychology, human weapons development cycles, and the particular way humans innovate under pressure. The Martians have not shared all of this with him either.

Venusians treat him poorly. He limits his exposure to Venusian command wherever possible.

## Trivia

- The clone count is finite. The Professor is the only person who knows the number. He has not told the Martians.
- He reads his own prior notes as source material for understanding himself. He marks passages he cannot verify as authentic memory with a private notation system he developed across several clone cycles.
- He has at no point used his cloning technology for anything other than his own continuation. The Martians have asked. He has declined.
